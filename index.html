Brandt van der Gaast

I teach philosophy and ethics at the University of Twente in the Netherlands. I'm an affiliate member of the 4TU Centre for Ethics and Technology.

Research

Areas of philosophy I'm interested in include ethics but also the philosophy of language.

In ethics, I'm interested in puzzles in population ethics. What are our obligations to future generations? Are the correct principles of the aggregation of well-being impersonal or person-affecting?

In the philosophy of language, my interests lie where questions of meaning and questions of mental content connect. How do linguistic and psychological intentionality relate? Meaning and mental content?

Teaching

For close to ten years, I've designed and taught courses in ethics: business ethics, introductory ethics, computer ethics, medical ethics and more. I like to think and talk about how to improve teaching technique and make it as appealing as possible to students.

Publications

Some papers in progress are: 'Why Does 'Green' Not Mean: Grue?', and 'Functionalism and Action Individuation'. Here is a link to my dissertation 'A Defense of Russellian Descriptivism', that I completed at the University of Massachusetts Amherst in 2014.
