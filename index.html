Teaching

For close to ten years, I've designed and taught courses in philosophy and ethics. From critical thinking to computer ethics, and from existentialism to the philosophy of mind. I like to think and talk about teaching techniques and about how to create appealing courses on a variety of subjects.

Research

Areas of philosophy I'm interested in include ethics but also the philosophy of language.

In ethics, I'm interested in puzzles in population ethics. What are our obligations to future generations? What are the correct principles of the aggregation of well-being?

In the philosophy of language, my interests lie where questions of meaning and questions of mental content connect. How do linguistic and psychological intentionality relate? How did David Lewis see this relation?.
