Brandt van der Gaast

I teach philosophy and ethics at the University of Twente in the Netherlands. I'm an affiliate member of the 4TU Centre for Ethics and Technology.

Research

Areas of philosophy I'm interested in include the philosophy of language and ethics.

In the philosophy of language, my interests lie where questions of meaning and questions of mental content intersect. How do linguistic and psychological intentionality relate? Meaning and mental content?

In ethics, I'm interested in puzzles in population ethics. What are our obligations to future generations? Are the correct principles of well-being aggregation impersonal or person-affecting?

Teaching

For close to ten years I've designed and taught courses in ethics: business ethics, introductory ethics, computer ethics, medical ethics and more. I like to think and talk about how to improve teaching and make it as appealing as possible to students.
